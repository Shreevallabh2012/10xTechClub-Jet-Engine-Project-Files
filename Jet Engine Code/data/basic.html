Introduction

A jet engine is a type of reaction engine that generates thrust by expelling high-speed gases. It is a cornerstone of modern aviation technology and has revolutionized air travel and aerospace engineering.

How Jet Engines Operate

Jet engines function based on Newton's Third Law of Motion: "For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction."

The primary stages include:

  • Intake: Air enters the front of the engine.
  • Compression: The air is compressed to increase its pressure.
  • Combustion: Fuel is introduced and ignited, producing high-temperature, high-pressure gas.
  • Exhaust: The hot gases are expelled at high speed, creating thrust.

Types of Jet Engines

  • Turbojet: The original jet engine design, optimized for high-speed flight.
  • Turbofan: Widely used in commercial aviation for efficiency and noise reduction.
  • Ramjet: Functions at supersonic speeds without rotating parts.
  • Scramjet: An advanced version of the ramjet, capable of hypersonic speeds.

Applications of Jet Engines

Jet engines are utilized in a wide range of high-performance systems, including:

  • Military fighter aircraft
  • Commercial passenger jets
  • Missile propulsion systems
  • Advanced aerospace vehicles
